Xtyle uses <strong> and <em> to give emphasis to words and sentences. The <b> and <i> tags are used for design elements.

Paragraphs

Paragraphs are defined using the <p> tag. Use paragraphs to separate your texts:

Proin tristique nunc enim, id scelerisque libero. Vestibulum viverra, tortor a vestibulum blandit, leo eros pellentesque mi, eu faucibus massa dolor ac lacus. Praesent et quam augue. In eget est nisi, et mattis ligula. Phasellus ut elit vel lorem faucibus molestie ac vehicula tortor.

Vivamus elementum tempus scelerisque. Maecenas id lectus orci, ut condimentum dui. Suspendisse eget dui eu nunc dignissim tincidunt at a massa. Phasellus feugiat fermentum adipiscing. Praesent pharetra ultrices sodales. Pellentesque sed diam nunc, sit amet auctor magna. 

<p>Proin tristique nunc ...</p>
<p>Vivamus elementum tempus ...</p>

Drop Caps

To capitalize the first word of your paragraph you can use drop caps. You can define a drop caps using the <span class="first-letter"> tag inside a paragraph:

Proin eu erat nunc, id viverra urna. Donec id quam ac lacus ultrices commodo quis id elit. Sed vitae diam nulla, ac convallis lacus. Nunc eu sapien quis tortor tempor ornare non eget massa. Nulla in eros odio, sed congue mi. Curabitur suscipit pulvinar hendrerit. Maecenas vestibulum nibh non erat auctor accumsan porta erat commodo. In dapibus volutpat nisl, ut egestas sem pellentesque ut. Nam tincidunt dapibus nunc non dapibus. Quisque a risus eros, tincidunt vulputate ante. Quisque pharetra aliquet eros a hendrerit. In non gravida nisl. Pellentesque nulla nisl, lobortis a euismod non, dapibus eu tellus. Nunc pellentesque metus et nisl euismod tincidunt. Vestibulum viverra rutrum massa in porta.

Blockquotes

Write quotes using the <blockquote> tag. Use paragraphs <p> inside blockquotes to write content on it. You can also include the source of your quote using the optional <cite> tag:

"A blockquote."

"With citation."

– by xchema 

<blockquote>
  <p>"A blockquote."</p>
  <cite>"by xchema"</cite>
</blockquote>

List styles

You can make your lists using the <ul> for unordered lists, <ol> for ordered lists, and <dt> for definition lists. Use <ul class="reset"> to remove styles from lists.

Unordered list:
  • Apples
    • Aceymac
    • Adanac
  • Oranges

<ul>
<li>Apples
  <ul><li>...</li></ul>
</li>
<li>Oranges</li>
</ul>
